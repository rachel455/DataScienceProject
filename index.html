Improvement in Air Quality

I have shown the claculated means of the pollutants for each borough. Proving the improvements in air quality over the years as the trends decrease. This is important because air quality affects everything around us, and air pollution has a negative impact on our bodies and lungs, especially young children and the elderly. Recognition of this will contribute to everyone's overall health.

Fine Particulate Matter

Fine Particulate Matter (PM2.5) are emitted by vehicles, building boilers, and other combustion - and are a major form of air pollution that harms health.

Black Carbon

Black Carbon is a major component of fine particulate matter (PM) in the air. Particulate matter is easily inhaled into the lungs and can contribute to both short-term acute health effects, as well as long-term chronic illness.

Nitric Oxide

Nitric Oxide (NO) is a group of pollutants formed by combustion that can cause damage to lung tissue, breathing and respiratory problems, as well as contribute to smog and acid rain.

Nitrogen Dioxide

Nitrogen Dioxide (NO2) is a group of pollutants formed by combustion that can cause damage to lung tissue, breathing and respiratory problems, as well as contribute to smog and acid rain.

Ozone

Ozone (O3) is a common air pollutant that can harm breathing and worsen asthma and other respiratory conditions.

Sulfur Dioxide

Sulfur Dioxide (SO2) - comes from burning certain types of fuel oil. As an air pollutant, it can worsen lung diseases.
